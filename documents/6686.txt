                     ASSISTANT/ASSOCIATE PROFESSOR OF ACCOUNTANCY (2)
                     
                     Position The School of Accountancy invites applications for a tenure-track assistant/associate
                        professor, preferably at the advanced level, effective fall semester 2017, pending
                        available funding. Responsibilities include effectively teaching undergraduate and
                        graduate courses, developing a stream of quality research, and fulfilling professional
                        and university service responsibilities. All specializations in accounting will be
                        considered. The School is an academically diverse, research-oriented group of 17 full-time
                        faculty members. The School offers degree programs at the bachelor's and master's
                        levels and is fully accredited by AACSB International. For more information about
                        the School, visit www.memphis.edu/accountancy .
                     
                     
                     Qualifications A Ph.D. in accounting from an AACSB-accredited institutions is required (advanced
                        ABDs may be considered if degree completion is imminent). Teaching and research interests
                        in all areas of accounting will be considered. All candidates must have either education
                        in, or experience working with, U.S. GAAP, tax law, and /or auditing standards. Effective
                        college teaching experience and evidence of strong research potential are required
                        for assistant professor. Applicants for associate professor must also have substantial
                        professional experience and a strong record of scholarly research. Professional certification
                        is desired (CPA preferred).
                     
                     
                     Compensation This is an academic-year appointment and offers competitive compensation, summer research
                        support, and an attractive benefits program, depending on qualifications.
                     
                     
                     The College and Department The Fogelman College of Business & Economics has become a leading business school
                        in the Southeast. Globalization is a strategic emphasis of the College. Degree programs
                        offered include the BBA, MA (Economics), MBA, Executive MBA, International MBA, MS,
                        and PhD. The academic programs of the College are fully accredited by AACSB International.
                        The College faculty of 96 serves over 3,000 students, with approximately 2,450 undergraduates,
                        525 master's students and 60 doctoral students. The highly qualified faculty is recognized
                        for scholarship and publications in leading academic and professional journals.
                     
                     
                     Application Application packages should include a cover letter outlining qualifications and interests,
                        detailed curriculum vitae, research sample, unofficial transcripts, and contact information
                        for three professional references, and be submitted online at: http://workforum.memphis.edu . Screening of applications will begin September 5, 2016 , and may continue until the position is filled. The Search Committee Chair is:
                     
                     
                     
                        
                        Dr. Kenton Walker, Director School of Accountancy Fogelman College of Business & Economics The University of Memphis Memphis, TN  38152-3120   901.678.4569, kbwalker@memphis.edu .

                     ASSISTANT/ASSOCIATE PROFESSOR OF BUSINESS INFORMATION AND TECHNOLOGY
                     
                     Position The Department of Business Information and Technology (formerly Management Information
                        Systems) invites applications for a tenure-track Assistant/Associate Professor, effective
                        fall semester 2017, pending available funding. Responsibilities include effectively
                        teaching undergraduate and graduate courses, developing a stream of quality research,
                        participating in activities of the Department, and fulfilling professional and university
                        service responsibilities. The Department is an academically diverse, professionally
                        active group of 16 full-time faculty members and offers the bachelor's, master's,
                        and doctoral degrees in information systems and supports other academic programs of
                        the Fogelman College.
                     
                     
                     Qualifications Candidates for Assistant Professor should have a Ph.D. with a concentration in information
                        systems or a closely related area from an AACSB-accredited university or international
                        equivalent (advanced ABDs considered if degree completion is imminent), a record of
                        effective college teaching and commitment to teaching excellence, and evidence of
                        development of a strong research program targeting top journals in the field. Candidates
                        should demonstrate an interest and/or background in technical topics of IS and applied
                        teaching with emphasis on software development technologies and/or data management.
                        A research competence in econometrics techniques is also strongly desired.
                     
                     
                     Effective college teaching experience and evidence of strong and continued research
                        potential are required. Applicants for Associate Professor must also have substantial
                        professional experience and a strong record of scholarly research. Experience in Ph.D.
                        student mentoring is highly desired.
                     
                     
                     Compensation This is an academic-year appointment and offers competitive compensation, summer research
                        support, and an attractive benefits program, depending on qualifications.
                     
                     
                     The College The Fogelman College of Business & Economics has become a leading business school
                        in the Southeast. Globalization is a strategic emphasis of the College. Degree programs
                        offered include the BBA, MA (Economics), MBA, Executive MBA, International MBA, MS,
                        and PhD. The academic programs of the College are fully accredited by AACSB International.
                        The College faculty of 96 serves over 3,000 students, with approximately 2,450 undergraduates,
                        525 master's students and 60 doctoral students. The highly qualified faculty is recognized
                        for scholarship and publications in leading academic and professional journals.
                     
                     
                     The University The University of Memphis is emerging as one of America’s great metropolitan research
                        universities. Founded in 1912, the University is a state, tax-assisted institution
                        with an enrollment of 20,600 students. The University is the flagship of the Tennessee
                        Board of Regents system and is one of two comprehensive doctoral-granting public institutions
                        in Tennessee, offering a broad array of degree programs at all levels. The University
                        has 24 Chairs of Excellence, more than any other Tennessee university, and five state-approved
                        Centers of Excellence. The modern and beautifully landscaped campus is centrally located
                        in an attractive residential area of east Memphis. For additional information about
                        the University, visit the Web site at: http://www.memphis.edu
                     
                     The Community Located in southwestern Tennessee on the banks of the Mississippi river, Memphis is
                        one of the South’s largest and most attractive cities. The population of Memphis is
                        about 650,000, with over 1.3 million in the metropolitan area. Memphis is home to
                        many nationally and internationally known companies, including three Fortune 500 companies:
                        FedEx Corporation, AutoZone, and International Paper. Memphis is a major distribution
                        center and boasts the world’s second busiest cargo airport. As a primary medical,
                        education, communication, and transportation center, Memphis offers a full range of
                        research opportunities and cultural experiences. The city, known worldwide for its
                        musical heritage, has many fine restaurants, theaters, and museums, as well as one
                        of the nation’s largest urban park systems. Memphis has one of the lowest costs of
                        living among metropolitan cities of 600,000 or more and enjoys a mild climate.
                     
                     
                     Application Application packages should include a cover letter outlining qualifications and interests,
                        detailed curriculum vitae, research sample, unofficial transcripts, and contact information
                        for three professional references, and be submitted online at: http://workforum.memphis.edu . Screening of applications will begin October 3, 2016 , and may continue until the position is filled. The Search Committee Chair is:
                     
                     
                     
                        
                        Dr. Bill Kettinger Professor and FedEx Chair of Excellence Department of Business Information and Technology 300 Fogelman Administration Building The University of Memphis Memphis, TN 38152   Voice:  901.678.4547;  Fax:  678-4151 Email:  wjkttngr@memphis.edu .

                     INSTRUCTOR OF BUSINESS INFORMATION AND TECHNOLOGY (2)
                     
                     Position The Department of Business Information and Technology in the Fogelman College of Business
                        & Economics at the University of Memphis is seeking qualified applicants for two Instructor
                        positions, beginning August 2017, pending available funding. These are nine-month,
                        non-tenure track appointments. The Instructor will teach primarily at the Memphis
                        campus though teaching at off-campus sites may be required. Responsibilities include
                        delivering effective instruction in a variety of undergraduate and graduate management
                        information systems (MIS) courses, evaluating student learning, fulfilling professional
                        and university service responsibilities, advising students, and complying with School,
                        College, and University regulations. Instructors normally teach four 3-credit courses
                        (12 hours) per semester; however reductions for research productivity will be considered.
                        In these positions, the Instructors will be responsible for teaching live and on-line
                        sections of courses including computer applications, critical thinking, and business
                        analytics.
                     
                     
                     Qualifications A Ph.D. in MIS or a related discipline is strongly desired. The minimum education
                        requirement is a master's degree in MIS or a closely related area. College teaching
                        experience is required, and online teaching experience is desired. Also, significant
                        related professional experience is desired.
                     
                     
                     Compensation This is an academic-year appointment and offers competitive compensation, summer research
                        support, and an attractive benefits program, depending on qualifications.
                     
                     
                     The College and Department The Fogelman College of Business & Economics has become a leading business school
                        in the Southeast. The academic programs of the College are fully accredited by AACSB
                        International. The College's full-time faculty of 96 serves approximately 3,165 students,
                        with nearly 2,550 undergraduates, 550 master's students, and 65 doctoral students.
                        The Department of Business Information and Technology has 16 full-time faculty members.
                        Additional information about the University, College, and Department is available
                        at the University's official website: http://www.memphis.edu .
                     
                     
                     Application Application packages should include a cover letter outlining qualifications and interests,
                        detailed curriculum vitae, research sample, unofficial transcripts, and contact information
                        for three professional references, and be submitted online at: http://workforum.memphis.edu . Screening of applications will begin November 1, 2016 , and may continue until the position is filled. The Search Committee Chair is:
                     
                     
                     
                        
                        Dr. Robin Poston Professor Department of Business Information and Technology 300 Fogelman Administration Building The University of Memphis Memphis, TN 38152   Voice:  901.678.4613;  Fax:  678-4151 Email:  rposton@memphis.edu .
