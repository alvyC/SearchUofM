                     SBS PhD Curriculum
                     
                     The SBS doctoral program is a 63 semester hour degree program, with 54 hours of graduate
                        coursework beyond the master's degree, and nine hours of PUBH 9000 for dissertation.
                        Students are required to fulfill prerequisite courses PUBH 8150 Biostatistical Methods
                        I, PUBH 8160 Social and Behavioral Science Principles, and PUBH 8170 Epidemiology
                        in Public Health I, or document their equivalent. Credit hours for these prerequisite
                        courses will not count toward the 63 hours required for graduation.
                     
                     
                     social and behavioral sciences core
                     
                     PUBH 8012 or 8014 Public Health Literacy or Public Health Communication PUBH 8130 Social Determinants of Health PUBH 8132 Health Program Evaluation PUBH 8340 Behavioral Intervention Development PUBH 8161 Advanced Psychosocial Theories in Health
                     
                     
                     research methods core
                     
                     A total of six (6) credit hours of quantitative courses and three (3) credit hours
                        of qualitative courses is required; students select two (2) of the following quantitative
                        courses, and one (1) of the following qualitative courses: Quantitative Courses HADM 8106 Health Services Research PUBH 8104 Large Data Sets in Public Health Research PUBH 8141 Epidemiologic Survey Methods PUBH 8172 Epidemiology in Public Health I PUBH 8339 Translational Research Methods in Population Health PUBH 8450 Randomized Clinical Trials PSYC 8301 Research Design and Methods Qualitative Courses  PUBH 8334 Community Based Participatory Research Methods PUBH 8347 Qualitative Methods in Health Research
                     
                     
                     biostatistics core
                     
                     PUBH 8152 Biostatistical Methods II; students select one (1) of the following courses
                        for a total of six (6) credit hours: PSYC 8302 Advanced Statistics PSYC 8304 Measurement Theory and Psychometrics PSYC 8305 Quantitative Methods of Review in Research PSYC 8306 Linear Structural Modeling PSYC 8308 Applied Multivariate Statistics PUBH 8310 Mixed Model Regression Analysis PUBH 8311 Applied Categorical Data Analysis 
                     
                     
                     Elective Courses
                     
                     A total of 18 credit hours is required; a minimum of two (2) elective courses must
                        be a PUBH course. Public Health Elective Courses PUBH 8800 Guided Research in Public Health PUBH 8140 Epidemiology of Chronic Disease PUBH 8333 Addictive Behaviors PUBH 8335 Structural and Environmental Issues in Urban Communities PUBH 8336 Women's Health PUBH 8337 Eating Behaviors, Nutrition and the Family PUBH 8338 Critical Issues in Global Health PUBH 8341 Physical Activity and Public Health PUBH 8342 Epidemiology of Minority and Ethnic Populations PUBH 8343 Tobacco Use:  Causes, Consequences, and Control PUBH 8345 Health Literacy PUBH 8346 Public Mental Health PUBH 8400 Special Problems PUBH 8447 Public Health Genomics Other Elective Courses COMM 8016 Health Communication Campaigns EDPR 8109 Infant Development EDPR 8110 Early Childhood Development EDPR 8165 Social Development in Children ESCI 8613 GIS and Human Health NUTR 8602 Community Nutrition NUTR  8212 Applied Nutrition for Health NUTR 8204 Life Span Nutrition PSYC 8207 Developmental Psychology PSYC 8217 Social Psychology PSYC 8416 Child Psychopathology PSYC 8506 Grant Writing SOCI 8851 Medical Sociology
                     
                     
                     doctoral seminars
                     
                     PUBH 8900 Advanced Concepts in Public Health I PUBH 8901 Advanced Concepts in Public Health II
                     
                     
                     dissertation
                     
                     PUBH 9000; up to nine (9) credit hours
                     
                     SBS phd program requirements
                     
                     In addition to completion of the 63 semester hours of required coursework, program
                        requirements include successful completion of written and oral comprehensive examinations,
                        and the preparation and successful defense of a dissertation in accordance with the
                        University of Memphis Graduate School policies and guidelines.
                     
                     
                     Questions about the SBS PhD Program curriculum and degree requirements?
                     
                     Satish Kedia, PhD, Professor and Coordinator Social and Behavioral Sciences Doctoral Program 901.678.1433 skkedia@memphis.edu
