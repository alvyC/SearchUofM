                     Operation Broken Silence!
                     
                     Former University of Memphis International Studies Major and current executive director
                        of Operation Broken Silence Mark Hackett was recently featured in the Memphis Flyer
                        for his film work covering the stories of Sudanese refugees. Mark was featured in
                        the Spring edition of our program's newsletter .
                     
                     
                     To read the article and learn more about Mark and Operation Broken Silence, follow
                        the link to the Flyer here . Additionally information about his organization can be found on their website at
                        www.operationbrokensilence.org .
                     
                     
                     Freeman-Asia Scholarship
                     
                     The University of Memphis with be hosting a workshop to learn about the Freeman-Asia
                        Scholarship which is intended for students studying abroad in East Asia.
                     
                     
                     The workshop will be held Wednesday, September 21st in the UC Beale Room at 1 p.m. 
                     
                     
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                      
                     
                     35th Annual Spindel Conference
                     
                     The Philosophy Department's signature and internationally renowned event will be help
                        on September 16th and 17th in the University Center. This year's theme is "Critical
                        Histories of the Present" which will bring together top scholars engaged in a critical
                        interrogation of the history of various aspects of the present, such as corporate
                        personhood, the politics of information, mass incarceration, and resistance and political
                        insurgency.
                     
                     
                     For a brief overview/poster of the event, please see this link .
                     
                     
                     For a detailed itinerary of the conference, please see this link .
                     
                     
                     College Republicans Present: Ending Modern Slavery
                     
                     On September 13th, the University of Memphis College Republicans will be hosting a
                        representative from the office of Senator Bob Corker to speak about the issues regarding
                        modern slavery and the initiatives taken by Senator Corker's proposals. It will take
                        place at 4 o'clock in the UC Bluff Room 304. Open invite and free food available!
                     
                     
                     For more information, please see the flier here .

                     International Research Travel Grants
                     
                     With the generous partnership of the Study Abroad Program, the Helen Hardin Honors Program , and Marcus Orr Center for the Humanities , we are able to fund two new research travel awards. For faculty-undergraduate research
                        partnerships in international settings, click here . For graduate students pursuing international humanities research, click here . 
                     
                     
                     Deadline has passed for 2016 awards (to see last year's recipients, please see our
                           " Resources " page). Grants will be offered again for the summer of 2017.
                     
                     Faculty seeking undergraduate research support should contact Keri Brondo with a brief
                           description of their research project – kbrondo@memphis.edu
