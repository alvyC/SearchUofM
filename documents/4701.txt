                     Dr. M. David Rudd Fund for the Benefit of U.S. Military Veterans
                     An anonymous donor who is a long-time supporter of the University of Memphis has created
                     the Dr. M. David Rudd Fund for the Benefit of U.S. Military Veterans with an initial
                     gift of $100,000. The gift was made in honor of Dr. Rudd's inauguration as the University's
                     12th president and his unwavering commitment to our country's veterans.
                     
                      
                     
                     Gifts to the Dr. M. David Rudd Fund for the Benefit of U.S. Military Veterans will
                        be used to support research devoted to veteran's studies, scholarships for veterans,
                        the University of Memphis Veteran's Resource Center , or any other program or initiative that benefits U.S. military veterans. Everyone
                        is invited to contribute to this important and meaningful fund for our veterans. Dr.
                        Rudd will be provided a list of individuals and organizations who make a gift in his
                        honor but not the amount of each gift.
                     
                     
                     Ways to Give
                     
                     By credit card : Click HERE to make your gift online using a credit card
                     
                     
                     By mail: Click HERE for a pledge form. Mail your completed form and check, make payable to the University
                        of Memphis Foundation, to:
                     
                     
                     Department 238 The UofM Foundation P.O. Box 1000 Memphis, TN 38148-0001
                     
                     
                     By phone: Call Lisa Mayne at 901-678-3953
                     
                     
                     Dr. M. David Rudd was named the 12th president of the University of Memphis on May
                        1, 2014. Throughout his career, he has been responsible in part for more than $18
                        million in research grant funding, including more than $3 million for research to
                        help military populations through the U.S. Army/Department of Defense Military Operational
                        Medicine Research Program. He has continued his affiliation with the National Center
                        for Veterans Studies at the University of Utah as co-founder and scientific director,
                        a role that has garnered much national attention. He has testified seven times before
                        the U.S. Congress on issues related to veterans and suicide.
