                     PhD students successfully defend dissertations, earn "Dr." title
                     
                     Congratulations to Blake C. Jones who successfully defended his doctoral dissertation entitled "The effect of predatory
                        encounters on HPA axis responsiveness and memory of avian prey" on Friday April 1st.
                        Dr. Jones performed his dissertation work in the laboratory of Dr. Stephan Schoech. Congratulations are also in order for Dr. Sara Bebus, who successfully defended her dissertation. The work presented in Dr. Bebus' dissertation
                        entitled "Individual differences in neophobia: relationships with stress-physiology,
                        experience, and learning" was also conducted in the laboratory of Dr. Stephan Schoech . Click here to learn more about the Schoech Lab .
                     
                     
                     Students receive recognition and fellowships for research

                        and fitness in carrot: implications for organellar genome evolution and gynodioecy"

                        resources, including supercomputing time, in support of research toward completion
                        of the graduate program of study. Adam is doing his research with Dr. Jennifer Mandel . We also wish to congratulateÂ  Malle Carrasco-Harris on her receipt of a Graduate Research Fellowship from the National Science Foundation.
                        Malle's proposal entitled "The Effects of Habitat Urbanization and Isolation on a
                        Top Predator" is being performed under the direction of Dr. Steve Reichling of the
                        Memphis Zoo and in collaboration with faculty in the Department of Biological Sciences.
