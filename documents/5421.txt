                        Biography
                        
                        David Spiceland is a Professor in the School of Accountancy where he is Director of
                           the Master of Science in Accounting Program and teaches Intermediate Accounting  in the undergraduate program and  Strategic Accounting  in the Master of Science Program. He serves on dissertation committees for PhD candidates
                           in the doctoral program.  Professor Spiceland received his B.S. degree in finance
                           from the University of Tennessee, his MBA from Southern Illinois University, and his
                           Ph.D. in accounting from the University of Arkansas. Dr. Spiceland also is a CPA.
                        
                        
                        Professor Spiceland is a textbook author whose several books include McGraw-Hill's
                           best-selling  Intermediate Accounting , now in its 8th edition, and  Financial Accounting ,in its 3rd edition.  
                        
                        
                        Dr. Spiceland has published articles in a variety of academic and professional journals
                           including  The Accounting Review ,  Accounting and Business Research , the  Journal of Financial Research , the  Journal of Accounting Education, and Issues in Accounting Education .

                        Professor Spiceland’s civic service activities include serving as chair of the board
                           for Kingsway Christian Church. He is founding co-president of the MidSouth Chapter
                           of Foundation Fighting Blindness. His professional service activities include affiliations
                           with professional organizations including the American Institute of Certified Public
                           Accountants, the American Accounting Association, the Institute of Management Accountants,
                           the Tennessee Society of Certified Public Accountants, and the Tennessee Society of
                           Accounting Educators.
