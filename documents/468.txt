                     Nominations Being Solicited for Lupfer Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching Award
                     
                     In 2012 Dr. Michael and Mrs. Shirley Lupfer made a generous endowment to establish the Lupfer Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching
                        Award. The award is given at four-year intervals to a fulltime member of the Psychology
                        faculty. Nominations are being sought from Psychology faculty, staff, alumni and current
                        students (both undergraduate and graduate).
                     
                     
                     If you would like to nominate someone, please visit this link to view criteria and submit a nomination. Deadline is November 23.
                     
                     
                     Dr. Helen Sable Honored for Teaching Excellence
                     
                     Dr. Helen Sable has received the W. Russell Smith Award for Teaching Excellence. The
                        selection of a recipient for this award is based primarily on nominations by graduating
                        seniors and is the College's highest honor for teaching excellence. Dr. Sable serves as director of the Psychology Undergraduate Program and is chair
                        of the Psychology Undergraduate Advisory Committee. She is a member of the College
                        of Arts and Sciences Undergraduate Curriculum Committee and University Undergraduate
                        Council. In 2012 Dr. Sable was selected as a recipient of the department's Lupfer Excellence
                        in Undergraduate Teaching Award.
                     
                     
                     Thinking about attending graduate school?
                     
                     We offer three doctoral programs: Clinical, Experimental and School Psychology and
                        two master’s degree programs: Master’s in General Psychology (MSGP) and a Master of
                        Arts/Educational Specialist in School Psychology. More information
                     
                     About the Department
                     
                     The University of Memphis Psychology Department consists of approximately 30 full-time
                        faculty members, 20 adjunct faculty (psychologists in the community who teach and/or
                        supervise our students), 5 postdoctoral fellows, 90 doctoral students, 58 masters
                        students, and over 700 undergraduate majors and minors.
                     
                     
                     The department is also the home of the Center for Applied Psychological Research,
                        one of the most successful of the centers created under the State of Tennessee's Centers
                        of Excellence program.
