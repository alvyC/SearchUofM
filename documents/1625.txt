                        About David Zettergren
                        
                        David G. Zettergren became the Vice President for Business & Finance in 2009. During
                           his 30+ years at the UofM, he has served as Manager of Financial Reporting, Director
                           of Accounting, Assistant Vice President for Finance, and Vice President for Business
                           & Finance. As vice president, he has responsibility for the University's fiscal, human,
                           and plant resources. He oversees an annual operating budget of more than $300 million
                           and 239 structures totaling 6.2 million square feet on 1,607 acres at eight locations.
                        
                        
                        Mr. Zettergren, a Certified Public Accountant, holds an undergraduate degree in Physical
                           Science and a master's degree in accountancy from the University of Memphis. Before
                           coming to the University, he worked as an audit supervisor for Ernst & Young LLP and
                           a research assistant at the University of Tennessee Center for Health Sciences.
                        
                        
                        Zettergren, who was named CFO of the Year in 2013 and received the Lifetime Achievement
                           Award in 2014 by the Memphis Business Journal, is a member of the Better Business
                           Bureau, the Southern Association of College and University Business Officers, and
                           the National Association of College and University Business Officers. He has served
                           on the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges Off-Site
                           Reaffirmation Committee and chaired the Tennessee Board of Regents Business Affairs
                           Sub-Council.
