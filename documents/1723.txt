                           About Kyle Yampiro
                           Kyle Yampiro is a native of Long Island, NY with a Doctor of Musical Arts degree with
                           a Singing Health emphasis from James Madison University in Harrisonburg, VA. As a
                           performer, he is equally at home in opera and musical theatre and has performed at
                           regional venues across the country, including the College Light Opera Company (MA),
                           Blue Gate Musicals (IN), Thin Air Theatre Company (CO), Luray Opera Theatre (VA),
                           and Texas Family Musicals. He has appeared multiple times as a guest artist at his
                           alma mater, the University of Delaware. Most recently, he appeared as a Resident Professional
                           Artist in the 2016 Ohio Light Opera season. Favorite roles include Tony Esposito (The
                           Most Happy Fella), Julian Marsh (42nd Street), Ko-Ko (The Mikado), Henry (Kern's Have
                           A Heart), and the title character in Puccini's Gianni Schicchi.
                           
                           As an educator, he last held an Adjunct Professor of Music position at Eastern Mennonite
                              University and has previously taught courses in audition prep, music theory, and music
                              appreciation at Shenandoah Valley Governor's School and JMU. Additionally, he has
                              served as music director, conductor, and assistant director for multiple productions,
                              director of scenes in JMU's Opera Guild program, and co-founder of the JMU Gilbert
                              & Sullivan Society. He has been inducted into Pi Kappa Lambda and Alpha Psi Omega,
                              music and theatre honor societies, respectively.
                           
                           
                           He has also arranged for and music directed award-winning a cappella groups: University
                              of Delaware's Vocal Point and JMU's Exit 245. His work with the latter group earned
                              a 2013 Contemporary A Cappella Recording Award (CARA) for Best Male Collegiate Album,
                              from which his solo track, "Cinema," earned inclusion on the Best of College A Cappella
                              (BOCA) 2013, Voices Only 2013, and sing 10: neon compilation albums. He remains an
                              active member of the a cappella community, where he writes reviews for the Recorded
                              A Cappella Review Board (RARB), teaches seminars at national festivals such as SoJam
                              and the Kettering National A Cappella Festival, and adjudicates competitions which
                              include the International Championship for Collegiate A Cappella (ICCA) and the CARAs.
