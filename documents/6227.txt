                     Job Positions
                     
                     
                     Instructor/Coordinator
                     		
                     
                     The Department of Mathematical Sciences invites applications for an instructor coordinator
                        position in Mathematics. Applicants must have either a
                        Ph.D. in Mathematical Sciences or a minimum of a Masters Degree in Mathematical Sciences
                        or a related field with at least 18 graduate hours in
                        Mathematics. Candidates should have a minimum of 2 years experience at the University
                        level, and a strong commitment to lower division undergraduate instruction. Experience
                        with MyMathLab or ALEKS software and NCAT redesign methods is desired. Normal duties
                        will include teaching undergraduate courses in
                        mathematics courses for Liberal Arts and Stem majors, and other administrative duties
                        which include: assisting with various aspects of the undergraduate
                        instructional program, advising undergraduate students, supervising graduate teaching
                        assistants, preparation of assessment and accreditation data, hiring
                        part-time faculty, etc. The department is especially interested in candidates who
                        are knowledgeable of higher education curriculum requirements and who have
                        experience with advising undergraduate mathematics students. It is intended to fill
                        up the position with a suitable candidate as soon as possible. Reviewing of applications
                        is ongoing and will continue until the position is filled.
                     
                     
                     Applications should be submitted electronically via workforum . Please upload the following: (1) curriculum vitae, (2) statement of teaching philosophy,
                        and request (3) reviewers to upload letters of recommendation, including one which
                        discusses the candidates teaching qualifications. A total of three letters
                        of recommendation along with a copy of the academic transcript should be uploaded.
                        E-mail questions to lasiecka@memphis.edu .
                     
                     	
                     		
                     The University of Memphis is a Tennessee Board of Regents Institution and an Equal
                        Opportunity/Affirmative Action Employer. We urge all qualified applicants to apply
                        for this position. Appointment will be based on qualifications as they relate to position
                        requirements without regard to race, color, national origin, religion, age, sex, disability
                        or veteran status. Successful candidates must meet guidelines of the Immigration and
                        Reform Control Act of 1986.
