                     LEAD Program Advisory Board
                     
                     Mission of the LEAD Program
                     
                     The University of Memphis strives to develop students to be leaders on our campus
                        and in our community, state and nation. The LEAD program teaches leadership skills
                        and helps shape a student's personal integrity and sense of civic responsibility through
                        leadership education, experiential learning and service opportunities.
                     
                     
                     LEAD Advisory Board Purpose
                     
                     The purpose of the Advisory Board is to provide counsel to University of Memphis administration
                        and staff who are responsible for the LEAD program to ensure that the programs are
                        of the highest quality and are preparing students to be leaders in business, government,
                        non-profit and civic organizations of all types.
                     
                     
                     The Advisory Board is composed of individuals who are leaders in their own communities
                        and organizations, who have demonstrated a personal commitment to serve, and who wish
                        to support the University of Memphis in its effort to educate students to be leaders.
                     
                     
                     The LEAD Advisory Board will:
                     
                     
                        
                        Provide counsel on new programs and changes in existing programs
                        
                        Identify leaders in the community to actively participate in various aspects of the
                           LEAD program
                        
                        
                        Assist in securing financial support for the LEAD program through gifts from alumni,
                           friends, businesses, corporations and foundations
                        
                        
                        Participate in programs with students and serve as advocates for the LEAD program
                           development
