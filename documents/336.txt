                     2016-2017 Theatre & Dance Season
                     
                     
                     Little Shop of Horrors A Sci-Fi Musical Book and Lyrics by Howard Ashman Music by Alan Menken Based on the film by Roger Corman, screenplay by Charles Griffith September 22-24 and 29-30, October 1
                     
                     
                     Tickets go on sale Monday, September 12 at 9am Feed the need for musical hilarity with this delicious sci-fi smash about a man-eating
                        plant. During a total eclipse, a meek flower shop assistant discovers an unusual plant,
                        which feeds only on human flesh and blood. The growing, carnivorous plant attracts
                        a great deal of business for the previously struggling flower shop. To keep the plant
                        happy and business hopping, Seymour must come up with bodies for the increasingly
                        bloodthirsty plant.
                     
                     
                     Measure for Measure A Dark Comedy by William Shakespeare November 3-5 at 7:30pm and November 6 at 2:00pm Tickets go on sale Monday, October 24 at 9am His city caught in a moral free-fall, the Duke of Vienna hands over power to Lord
                        Angelo, who enforces long-dormant codes of chastity with zealous fervor. When a pious
                        young novice pleads for the life of her condemned brother, Angelo's response is surprisingly
                        sensual—revealing a web of desire, deception and hypocrisy that infects every corner
                        of society.
                     
                     
                     Momentum A Faculty and Guest Choreographers' Dance Concert at the Rose Theatre November 11 at 7:30pm November 12 at 2:00pm and 7:30pm Tickets go on sale Monday, October 31 at 9am
                     
                     The Sparrow A Coming of Age Mystery by Chris Mathews, Jake Minton and Nathan Allen November 18 at 7:30pm and November 19 at 2:00pm Tickets go on sale Monday, November 7 at 9am After surviving a horrible accident, Emily must return to school her senior year.
                        As she navigates the realities of the high school halls, she discovers she has special
                        powers. The Sparrow taps into the fundamental aching of adolescence – fearing that
                        we are somehow different while hoping that we are somehow special.
                     
                     
                     Empires of Eternal Void A Psychological Thriller by Kenley Smith November 19 at 7:30pm and November 20 at 2:00pm Tickets go on sale Monday, November 7 at 9am Locked in a transparent cell with a seemingly delusional young soldier, a military
                        chaplain attempts to learn the truth about a violent, shocking crime. But as the session
                        unfolds, punctuated by threats and even torture, the chaplain must battle his own
                        conscience and past trauma as he tries to reach the tormented prisoner in the box.
                     
                     
                     Anon(ymous) A Modern Retelling of The Odyssey by Naomi Iizuka February 16-18 and 23-25 Tickets go on sale Monday, February 6 at 9am Separated from his mother, a young refugee called Anon journeys through the United
                        States, encountering a wide variety of people – some kind, some dangerous and cruel
                        – as he searches for his family. From a sinister one-eyed butcher to beguiling barflies
                        to a sweatshop, Anon must navigate through a chaotic, ever-changing landscape in this
                        entrancing adaptation of Homer's Odyssey.
                     
                     
                     New Voices An Emerging Choreographers' Dance Concert in CFA124 April 6-9 Thursday-Saturday evening performances at 7:30pm, Saturday and Sunday matinees at
                        2:00pm Tickets go on sale Monday, March 27 at 9am
                     
                     Spring Awakening A Rock Musical Book and Lyrics by Steven Sater Music by Duncan Sheik Based on the play by Frank Wedekind April 13-15 and 20-22
                     
                     
                     Tickets go on sale Monday, April 3 at 9am A rock musical based on the German play Spring Awakening (1891) by Frank Wedekind.
                        Set in late-19th-century Germany, Spring Awakening explores the journey from adolescence
                        to adulthood with poignancy and passion that is illuminating and unforgettable. The
                        landmark musical is an electrifying fusion of morality, sexuality and rock & roll.
                     
                     
                     The fall 2016 box office hours are...
                     
                     Monday 9:00am to 4:00pm Tuesday 9:00am to 10:30am and 12:00 to 4:00pm Wednesday 10:00am to 4:00pm Thursday 9:00am to 10:30am and 12:00 to 4:00pm Friday 9:00am to 2:00pm The box office opens at 6:30 on performance nights. We ask that you pick up your tickets by 7:00pm on the night of the performance.
