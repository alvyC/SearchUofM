                     Welcome to Human Subject Protection
                     
                     The University of Memphis Institutional Review Board (IRB) is a committee designed
                        to approve, monitor, and review any research involving humans. The aim of the IRB
                        is to protect the rights and welfare of the research subjects.
                     
                     
                     This web site is designed to guide you through the process of obtaining approval for
                        human subject research. The Education Requirements page links to where you can obtain certification to conduct research involving human subjects. Effective 6/30/12, certification is
                        required for the investigator and, if applicable, the faculty advisor before the IRB
                        will process your application. Please include the CITI certification number in your
                        application.
                     
                     
                     
                     
                     cayuse irb
                     
                     On August 1, 2016 Cayuse IRB will be ready for use. Researchers, please create new/initial protocols
                        using Cayuse IRB starting August 1st.
                     
                     
                     *************************************************************************** For Information on training and using Cayuse IRB, click here . ***************************************************************************
                     
                     
                     From Thursday, August 25th – Wednesday, August 31st the IRB will not be accepting protocols for review so that Cayuse IRB software implementation can
                        be finalized.
                     
                     
                     On September 1, 2016 , it will be mandatory for all UofM researchers to use Cayuse IRB for all protocol submissions. 
                     
                     
                     
                     
                     See IRB Forms to download the most current applications, final/progress reports, template consent
                        forms and other important documents.
                     
                     
                     See the FAQ for general questions. The Decision Guide can help you answer questions such as: Am I involved in research? Does the research
                        involve Human Subjects?
                     
                     
                     If you need to report a study-related injury, or if you have questions, please contact
                        the IRB Administrator at irb@memphis.edu or 901-678-2705.
                     
                     
                     If you have any concerns regarding integrity and ethics in research and scholarship
                        at UofMemphis, please do not hesitate to call 901-678-2705 or email irb@memphis.edu . The confidentiality of anyone who contacts the office will be protected under the
                        Whistleblower Protection Act. Working together we will promote the highest standard
                        of integrity and ethics in research and scholarship.
                     
                     
                     IRB Administrator Office of Sponsored Programs University of Memphis 315 Admin Bldg Memphis, TN 38152-3370 irb@memphis.edu 901-678-2705
